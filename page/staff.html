Our Clinical Staff

At Resolve, our clinical staff members are the heart of the services we provide. Our licensed counselors are dedicated to offering compassionate care, rooted in evidence-based practices. With a wide range of expertise in various therapeutic approaches, our clinicians work with individuals, couples, and families to provide the support they need to achieve lasting change.

Meet Our Clinical Team

Lidia D. Abrams
PhD, Executive Director and Clinical Supervisor

Licensed Psychologist (NJ & NY)
Diplomate, Board of Rational Emotive/Cognitive Behavior Therapy
Fellow & Supervisor, Albert Ellis Institute

Dr. Lidia Abrams is an accomplished Licensed Psychologist in both New Jersey and New York with extensive experience in clinical supervision, therapy, and mental health education. As Executive Director and Clinical Supervisor of Resolve Community Counseling Center, Dr. Abrams leads the center's mission of providing compassionate and professional mental health services. Key Qualifications & Expertise:

  • Training with Dr. Albert Ellis, the founder of Rational Emotive Behavioral Therapy (REBT), providing a deep foundation in evidence-based therapeutic approaches.
  • Co-author of articles and books on various psychological topics, contributing to the advancement of knowledge in the field.
  • Member of the New Jersey Psychological Associationâ€™s Ethics Committee, ensuring adherence to ethical standards in the field.

Professional Contributions:

  • Board Member of the New Jersey Association for Family and Conciliation Courts (AFCC), working on improving family court processes in New Jersey.
  • Expert Witness for the Division of Child Protection and Permanency (DCPP) and Forensic Evaluator for Union County Family Court.
  • Honored as Person of Special Value by the Union County Mental Health Association for her dedication to mental health advocacy.

Dr. Abrams' leadership, clinical expertise, and commitment to ethical practice make her a trusted resource for clients and professionals alike.

Dr. Debra Ferro
LMFT, PhD, Director of Clinical Training

Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist (LMFT)

Dr. Debra Ferro is a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist (LMFT) in New Jersey and has been a valued member of the Resolve Community Counseling Center team for over 10 years. With a rich background in both education and clinical practice, Dr. Ferro brings a unique and comprehensive approach to her work as Director of Clinical Training. Learn More

Key Qualifications & Expertise:

  • Graduate degrees in Human and Organizational Psychology and Marriage and Family Therapy, as well as a Doctorate in General Psychology.
  • Research focused on the relationships between parenting styles, adolescent identity, and social media addiction in young adults, contributing valuable insights to the field.
  • Certified in Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT), enhancing her ability to treat individuals with mood and personality disorders.

Professional Contributions:

  • Adjunct Professor at Union County College, teaching courses on Child and Adolescent Psychology and Life Span Development.
  • Nominated for the Dr. Richard F. and Barbara Neblett Prize for Educational Excellence in Teaching and Learning for her outstanding contributions to education.
  • Presented on topics including adolescent stress, stress management in the workplace, and Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) at various conferences and as a guest lecturer at Kean University.
  • Facilitated support groups on children of divorce, social skills for middle schoolers, and parenting children with special needs.

Roles at Resolve:

  • Carries a large caseload of individuals, families, and children/adolescents, providing therapeutic services and guidance.
  • Conducts custody and best interest evaluations, visitation risk assessments, and provides court testimony when necessary.
  • Supervises interns and professionals working toward their licensure, providing mentorship and training to the next generation of clinicians.

In light of the recent developments with the COVID-19 pandemic, Resolve Community Counseling Center is discontinuing in-person counseling sessions as of Monday, March 23, 2020 until further notice. Office staff will continue to respond to phone calls (908 322 9180) and emails resolve@resolvenj.com. Our intake process is very similar to our in person process. We have a large, qualified and caring clinical staff here to address the mental health needs of the community. If you need help or know someone who is lonely or in need of support, please reach out to us.
